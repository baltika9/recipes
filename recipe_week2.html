Waldorf Salad

This recipe works well with many common Thanksgiving and Christmas entrees.

Source:

Ingredients

  • 1-1/2 cups mayonnaise
  • 3 tablespoons sugar
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 9 medium red apples, cored and diced
  • 3 cups sliced celery
  • 1-1/2 cups chopped walnuts

Preparation

  1. In a small bowl, combine the mayonnaise, sugar, lemon juice, and salt. Mix until smooth.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the apples and celery.
  3. Pour the mayonnaise mixture over the apples and celery and toss until coated.
  4. Cover and chill for 30 minutes. Just before serving, toss in the walnuts.

Caesar Salad

Caesar salad is an excellent first course for a dinner party.

Source:

Ingredients

  • 1 egg
  • 2 tablespoons water, milk, or chicken broth
  • 1 garlic clove
  • 1/2 teaspoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 tablespoon anchovy paste, or 3 anchovies, finely minced
  • 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 tablespoon white wine vinegar
  • 5 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • 6 cups romaine lettuce leaves, whole or torn
  • 1 cup salad croutons

Preparation

  1. Beat the egg and combine it with the water in a small, heavy pan.
  2. Cook the mixture over low heat, stirring constantly with a spoon for about 4 minutes or until the egg thickens into a foamy mass and coats a metal spoon with a thin egg layer. This process provides a safe alternative to a coddled egg (a Caesar salad standard).
  3. Remove the egg from the pot and place it in a blender with the garlic clove, Worcestershire sauce, anchovy paste, 1 tablespoon Parmesan cheese, the vinegar, oil, and pepper.
  4. Turn the blender on and process about 30 seconds or until the mixture is creamy.
  5. Place the lettuce leaves in a large serving bowl.
  6. Add the croutons and toss.
  7. Pour on the dressing.
  8. Toss gently but well.
  9. Sprinkle the remaining 3 tablespoons of Parmesan cheese over the salad.

Caprese Salad

This classic Italian salad works well as a light lunch or as an appetizer.

Source:

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cups arugula, washed (optional)
  • 2 large ripe tomatoes, cut into 1⁄4-inch-thick slices
  • 3⁄4 pound mozzarella, cut into 1⁄4-inch-thick slices
  • 8 leaves basil, finely sliced, or 2 tablespoons dried oregano

Preparation

  1. Prepare the dressing, whisking together the vinegar and salt and pepper in a small mixing bowl. Slowly whisk in the oil until the dressing is smooth. Set aside.
  2. Line 4 plates with the arugula leaves. Arrange alternating slices of tomato and mozzarella over the arugula to form a circle.
  3. Sprinkle the salad with basil and drizzle with the prepared dressing. Serve immediately after the salad is dressed.
